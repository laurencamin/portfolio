My Journey

My journey to become a Software Developer began when I entered a design sprint during the COVID-19 pandemic. The design sprint required participants to submit ideas to help schools reopen and assist the emotional, mental, and physical health of students, educators, and additional constituents. My idea was a software to help recipients ask for assistance from nonprofit organizations without compromising their privacy. After my submission, I discovered my idea was ranked as one of the top ideas in the design sprint. The design sprint challenged me to think about how I could use my knowledge and skills to make the world a more equitable place for everyone.

Later on, I attempted to create the software by using no-code and low-code software. However, I realized I wanted to add more functionality to the software and needed additional training to do so.

After completing a software engineering bootcamp, I have acquired skills in using JavaScript, HTML, CSS, React, MongoDB, Mongoose, and Node.js.
